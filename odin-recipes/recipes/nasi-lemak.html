Recipe for Nasi Lemak

Description

Nasi Lemak is a fragrant and flavorful dish originating from Malaysia, often considered the country's national dish. It consists of coconut milk-infused rice served with a variety of accompaniments, such as crispy anchovies, roasted peanuts, hard-boiled or fried egg, cucumber slices, and a spicy sambal (chili paste). It can also be paired with additional proteins like fried chicken, beef rendang, or squid. The dish is known for its rich, creamy rice and the perfect balance of sweet, savory, and spicy flavors.

Ingredients

Rice:

  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups long grain rice, rinsed and drained
  • 1 (1/2 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 whole bay leaf
  • salt to taste

Garnish:

  • 1 cup oil for frying
  • 1 cup raw peanuts
  • 1 (4 ounce) package anchovies, washed
  • 4 large hard-boiled eggs, peeled and halved
  • 1 medium cucumber, sliced

Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 3 medium shallots, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 2 teaspoons chili paste
  • 1 tablespoon water, or more as needed (Optional)
  • 1 (4 ounce) package anchovies, washed
  • 1/4 cup tamarind juice
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • salt to taste

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Making the rice. Stir coconut milk, water, rice, fresh ginger, ground ginger, bay lead, and salt together in a medium saucepan.
  3. Cover and bring to a boil over medium heat. Reduce the heat and simmer until tender, 20 to 30 minutes. Discard bay leaf and keep rice warm until garnish and sauce are ready.
  4. While the rice is cooking, make the garnish: Heat 1 cup vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Stir in peanuts and cook briefly, until lightly browned. Remove peanuts with a slotted spoon and place onto paper towels to soak up excess grease.
  5. Return the skillet to the stove. Stir in anchovies and cook, turning occasionally, until crisp, 2 to 3 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and place on paper towels. Discard oil and wipe out the skillet.
  6. Making the sauce. Heat oil in the clean skillet. Stir in onion, shallots, and garlic; cook until fragrant, 1 to 2 minutes.
  7. Mix in chili paste and cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally; if mixture is too dry, add water as needed.
  8. Stir in anchovies and cook for 5 minutes. Stir in tamarind juice, sugar, and salt; simmer until sauce thickens, 5 to 10 minutes.
  9. Ladle warm rice into bowls. Top with the sauce, peanuts, fried anchovies, hard-boiled eggs, and cucumber slices.
